  • MymmGO

    Application


    Overview

    This was my first mobile application that was going to be implemented with the flutter framework, so I sticked with Material Design 3 design system. MymmGO is an app that is used by salesmen, remote workers, drivers, production managers and generally people that need to record their work, tasks, worktime etc. from their tablet devices.



    Problem statement

    In the company we have an ERP system to organize everything, but it was only accessible from desktop devices. So, quickly a need for mobile version of it occurred by the people that don’t always work 9-5 or they don’t have a base and they are always on the move. Extra requirements came along with that because the desktop ERP system didn’t fulfill their needs.


    Research and Discovery

    When this project was presented to me, I had a difficult time understanding it because I had to learn the whole workflow of these people, their special needs and pain points. But no one is more suitable to explain their work than the workers themselves, so I insisted to have a meeting with one of them to save time from speculations. We sat down and discussed their typical day at work and generally how could this app save them time and effort in simple things. That helped a lot and it was time to dive in the design process.



    Design

    For the look and feel of this app I adapted our prior ERP system’s color palette, typography and icons in order to make the transition easier through familiarity. I used as much negative space as I could to make the responsiveness easier because everyone use their personal tablets rather than the companie’s. I also used color semantics for different stages of the production to help separate them and understand them with a quick look.



    Challenges

    First and foremost was checking in and out to record their work time, so it all begins after they check in. I included the concept of notifications because many warnings (system messages, human errors, reminders etc.) needed to be acknowledged in time to be resolved. Furthermore, a history of their daily tasks was helpful because they can iterate them at the end of the day in hectic weeks. Another critical point is that many production managers pick up their coworkers’ unfinished tasks and progress them or complete them entirely. Because of that, all the production process needed to be recorded with a certain timeline so each manager could interfere and help progress the tasks. A help corner was also necessary because every new user that was not familiar with all this workflow needed a little nudge.



    Reflection

    This project was a huge learning step for me because it was the first time that I used a design system, sat down and talked with the user and tried to understand their needs and pain points and lastly fragmented a complex process to make it easy and straight forward.

  • Online Listening Audio Files

    Web Design


    Overview

    This project was my very first one in my work. The task was to redesign a mini site that was used by teachers and students to hear listening exercises. These mini sites were different for each one of the English books’ series, but they all served the same purpose.



    Problem statement

    The previous one was an old fashioned, non-functional, non-responsive site that gave everyone hard time when they used it. So, the main goal was clear, but as always, I took it a little bit further. I thought that designing just this site could lead to changing all the listening audio sites. I came up with the idea of making something that could work as a template and with a very few changes it can visually fit all the English books series and solve the problem once and for all.


    Research and Discovery

    I was searching for music platforms, music players, audio records and nothing fitted what I had in mind. Then, skipping the song that I was listening at that time gave me the solution! The inspiration I was searching for was the very music platform that I use every day and admire the most, Spotify!



    Design

    I used only black and white to be as neutral as I could. The trick to fit all themes is that I played with opacity and background blur on every component. This way, by replacing the background with the corresponding book’s cover we get a whole new look and feel. It is based on Bootstrap 5.3 using its components and grid system strictly down to the last pixel.


    Reflection

    This project introduced me to the designer-developer relationship because before that, everything I did was either developed by me or never developed. I did a lot of mistakes at first and the developers kept bringing back my designs because they lacked consistency and details. It was at this moment that I realized that UI design was not only about fun and aesthetic interfaces but also pixel perfect detailing and strict rules.

  • Seminars app

    Application


    Overview

    This is one of my favorite projects because I collaborated closely with a programmer colleague and together we encouraged each other to use techniques and methods that we haven’t used before. It is a simple application that is used by the front desk people in events and seminars to manage with the participants list and check them in and out.



    Problem statement

    Before we made this application our agents in the events used to handle the participants lists with pen and paper, but this quickly led to dead end because handwritten papers usually got lost, the update in database was delayed, there was not an easy way to keep statistics. The overall user experience was terrible and they needed a big change.


    Research and Discovery

    In the first place, I was told to make a simple app that included a QR scanner and a simple UI just to cover that need quickly. But I don’t work this way because I know that later this app will be used in larger scale and I had to make good fundamentals in order to support needs that will occur in the future. I researched in already published apps to study how they managed with participants. I also talked with the backend developer that already had designed the database architecture and the API to clarify what information we have and how we can use it.


    Design

    I started the ideation process along with the low fidelity wireframes and found out that the API calls and the database architecture was missing critical methods like keeping track on how many participants are checked in and how many are not, undo methods (in cases of miss clicks and human errors), status of participants etc. So, with the support of my developer colleague, we convinced the managers and the seniors to change the perspective they had for this app and adopt ours. So, the first wireframes were made and they liked the features very much.



    Final Design

    For the high-fidelity prototypes I made a simple yet friendly interface with colors from the color palette of the brand, added micro animations, floating CTA buttons and a drawer effect to visually help the user understand the navigation and in which step of the process they are in. I included some tips for the QR scanner to unstuck the user from a difficult situation of scan malfunctions, I added an extra option to check in someone that may have forgotten their ticket and wanted to validate via ID and lastly insisted very much on creating a sign-in form to prevent spreading the APK and maybe falling in the wrong hands.


    Reflection

    When I finished the designs and finally prepared the launch for the developer, we sat together and discussed about how we are going to make it. We pushed his developer skills to the limit and we ended up with a beautiful result that pleased both our managers and the users because of its usability, features and the capability to support further features in the future.
